Isobaric Compression

The refrigerant gas is compressed at constant pressure, reducing its volume and increasing its temperature (initially), then heat is released.
  • If a piston or external force compresses a gas, the volume (V) decreases.
  • ΔV is negative, and therefore W is negative, indicating work is done on the gas.
  • Since compression reduces the temperature of the gas, the internal energy decreases (ΔU < 0).
  • Heat is lost from the system (Q < 0) because energy decreases both in internal energy and work.
W=PΔV Q=ΔU+W
Q < 0: Heat is removed.
W < 0: Work is done on the gas.
ΔU < 0: Internal energy decreases.
ΔU= 32nRΔT = 32NkBΔT
